Turntable Automator

I automated the creation of a turntable for an asset for both modeling and surfacing. This tool first reads an XML file that has pre-set settings for the turntable. In this, for each discipline, there are two different quality options, either low or high quality. The tool will show either the modeling settings or the surfacing settings depending on the discipline in which the asset was opened. At any point, the XML file can be changed to have new settings and these will automatically apply to the turntable. Here's an example of part of the XML settings file:

Tool Steps

After opening the asset the artist wants to create a turntable for, they can open the turntable tool from within our pipeline and choose the quality they would like. Within modeling, low quality means playblasting the proxy, whereas high is the render. Here's an example of what it looks like in modeling:

The artist can then click create, which will playblast the turntable and save the turntable video both locally and on our network (where all of our project files are stored) in that asset's directory of official files. Note that, in modeling, two playblasts are made: one with wireframe and one without. These are then merged together into one video to create the final turntable.

Within surfacing, there are still two different quality options for the turntable but the settings are for rendering each frame of the turntable. Again, the artist clicks create, which will render the surfacing frames, combine all the frames together to create the video, then save the turntable video both locally and on our network in that asset's directory of official files.
